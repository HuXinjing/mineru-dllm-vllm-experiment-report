1 Introduction

In recent years, Vision-Language Models. (VLMs) [1, 6, 44, 59, 11, 15, 56, 55, 28] have become the dominant paradigm for document Optical Character Recognition (OCR) [29, 17, 16, 47, 7]. These models encode textual images into visual representations and generate structured text through left-right autoregressive decoding [41], achieving strong performance across benchmarks [32, 9, 42]. Despite architectural unification and scaling, the decoding process remains strictly sequential. This design introduces efficiency and reliability bottlenecks when parsing long documents and complex layouts, particularly in highly structured scenarios such as tables and formulas [32].

From the perspective of task formulation, a high-quality OCR system should primarily depend on authentic visual evidence to perform character-level recognition, rather than relying on semantic completion from a language model.
